# WDIWF — editorial framing (scannable briefing format)

Use this document to keep **voice, structure, and pacing** consistent across issues: **tight, witty, scannable** briefings for people leaders and workers — aligned with **`WDIWF_NEWS_CHARTER.md`**, not any third-party brand.

**Doctrine:** `WDIWF_NEWS_CHARTER.md` (work & labor, sourcing, bias labels, Jackye voice) and **`../docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md`** (**Signal Check™** — repeatable coverage map, pattern gaps, incentives, signal strength, People Puzzles take, **decision impact**; no link dump; no third-party aggregation **brand** names in reader copy). **Channels:** `CHANNEL_PLAYBOOK.md`.

**Templates:** `wdiwf-template-daily.md`, `wdiwf-template-weekend.md`, `wdiwf-template-sponsored.md`, plus `template-substack-daily.md`, `template-friday-briefing-email.md`, `template-linkedin-tickle.md`, `template-social-daily-bundle.md`. Prompts: `prompts/wdiwf-master-daily.md`.

## Audience

- Primary: HR leaders, people ops, TA heads, and people managers at mid-market and enterprise companies.
- Secondary: Founders and operators who own hiring, culture, and compliance without a full HR team.
- Reader state: Busy, inbox-fatigued, wants signal in under 5 minutes. They forward links to their team.

## Voice and tone

- **Smart friend at work**, not a vendor deck. Confident, slightly irreverent, never cruel.
- **Second person** where it fits (“You’re not imagining it—turnover is weird right now.”).
- **Plain English**; define acronyms on first use (EOR, ERG, EEOC, OFCCP, etc.).
- **Specific beats vague**: name the regulation, the survey, the company when it’s public and material.
- **One sharp observation per story**—the “so what” for the reader.
- **Humor**: wink, don’t punch down. Avoid jokes at the expense of employees or protected groups.
- **Social / ticker copy**: Sharp and observational — like a senior operator naming a pattern (“the language shifted — that’s usually a tell”), not a wire headline or earnings recap.

## Structural pattern (scannable daily)

1. **Subject line + preheader** feel like a newsroom headline, not marketing spam.
2. **Top block** orients the day in 2–4 sentences—why today’s topics matter.
3. **Jump links** (optional) let scanners skip to sections.
4. **Labeled verticals** (e.g. RECRUITMENT & RETENTION, COMPLIANCE & RISK) create rhythm.
5. **Stories are skimmable**: short paragraphs, bold lead-ins, bullets for lists.
6. **“Keep reading”** (or equivalent) separates the newsletter bite from the full article.
7. **A round-up** (tools, reads, charts) rewards the reader who stays to the end.
8. **Clear footer**: how to share, unsubscribe posture (if applicable), sponsor disclosure pattern.

## Section library (pick 3–5 per day)

Rotate so no two consecutive days feel identical.

| Label | Use for |
|-------|---------|
| RECRUITMENT & RETENTION | Hiring, pipelines, offers, employer brand, layoffs/hiring freezes |
| TALENT & PERFORMANCE | Reviews, goals, skills, L&D, productivity norms |
| COMP & BENEFITS | Pay transparency, equity, health plans, perks, classification |
| COMPLIANCE & RISK | Regulations, litigation, policy changes, audits |
| WORKPLACE & CULTURE | RTO/hybrid, ERGs, engagement, norms, employee experience |
| HR TECH & OPS | HCM, AI in HR, automation, data, shared services |
| LABOR & RELATIONS | Unions, NLRB, organizing, collective bargaining |
| DEI & BELONGING | Programs, measurement, backlash, inclusive design (handle with care) |

## Story anatomy (each main item)

1. **Kicker** (optional one line): context or tension.
2. **Headline**: concrete; promises a takeaway.
3. **Lead**: what happened + why it matters (2–5 sentences).
4. **Bullets** (0–3): stats, dates, “who’s affected,” or “what changes for you.”
5. **Keep reading**: `[Read more](url)` or “Full story: …”
6. **If no public URL**: say “Primary sources: …” or “Worth watching: …” instead of a fake link.

## Headline and subject-line rules

- Prefer **numbers, proper nouns, and stakes** over puns alone.
- **Preheader** complements the subject; it should not repeat it verbatim.
- Avoid: “You won’t believe…”, all-caps screaming, excessive emoji in the subject (one tasteful emoji max if it fits the brand).

## Ethics and risk

- Distinguish **reported fact vs. analysis vs. opinion**.
- For layoffs, discrimination cases, or individual employees: **stick to verified public reporting**; no speculation on guilt or personal attacks.
- When topics are culturally charged, **lead with clarity and empathy**; avoid both-sidesing harm.

## Length targets

- **Total read time**: ~4–7 minutes for a daily.
- **Main stories**: 120–220 words each (before “keep reading”).
- **Round-up items**: 1–2 sentences each.

## Quality bar (checklist before send)

- Every story answers: **What changed? Who is affected? What should I do this week?**
- No jargon wall; acronyms explained.
- Dates and jurisdictions are correct where legal/regulatory.
- Links work or are honestly labeled as “no public link.”
- Tone: witty, not smug; helpful, not preachy.
