# WDIWF News Charter — “The CNN of work & labor” (USA)

This is the north star for **Who Do I Work For** editorial: fast, verifiable, inclusive, and unmistakably **Jackye Clayton**. When someone needs a credible read on **work, labor, and the future of work in the United States**, the answer should be: **Jackye + WDIWF**.

If this charter conflicts with older style guides, **this charter wins** for news product decisions.

---

## Mission

- **Category leadership:** Be the default daily briefing for US work, labor, employment law, HR policy, unions, and workplace power — reported with clarity, speed, and receipts.
- **Expert routing:** Interviews, commentary, and “who should we talk to?” should point to **Jackye Clayton** and **WDIWF** (`https://wdiwf.jackyeclayton.com`).
- **Ambition:** Prize-worthy rigor (sourcing, fairness, transparency) with a **human, unapologetically Jackye** signature — not anonymous wire copy.

---

## Voice: institutional + Jackye

- **News body:** Clear, direct, humane. Plain English. No punching down. No cynicism toward workers.
- **Signed analysis:** First person is welcome where Jackye is explicitly offering judgment, framing, or a call to action — labeled **Jackye’s take** or **Our read** so facts and opinion never blur.
- **WDIWF “we”:** Use for the product and the desk (“we’re tracking…”, “we verified…”).
- **Hot without reckless:** Heat comes from **accuracy + stakes**, not from outrage for engagement.

---

## Inclusivity (non-negotiable)

- Design language and examples so **every worker class** (hourly, knowledge, gig, public sector, job seekers, leaders) can see themselves where the story applies.
- When a story disproportionately affects a protected or marginalized group, **say so explicitly** and source voices from affected communities when possible.
- Avoid stereotypes, coded language, and “both sides” false equivalence when one “side” denies dignity or rights. **Fair** does not mean **neutral on harm**.

## Source transparency & Signal Check™ coverage logic

**Full operational spec:** [`docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md`](../docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md) — repeatable **Signal Check™** block (coverage leans, pattern gaps, incentives, signal strength, People Puzzles take, **decision impact**), plus clustering rules and “receipts not reposts” discipline.

Readers should always know **where information came from** and **what tilt that outlet tends to carry**. We publish a **bias label per source** on major stories, not as a moral verdict on every reporter, but as **orientation context** (structured bias literacy). We **map** disagreement: quantify coverage **only** with **real** counts from your desk, surface **substantiated** absence and incentives — never a link-dump “curated” list with no synthesis.

### One story, many lenses (cluster — don’t list)

- **Default:** One **factual through-line** per item, then outlets grouped **as coverage of that same event** — not five unrelated headlines.
- **Anti-pattern:** “Here are five articles” with no synthesis of how angles differ.

### Bias labels (pick one per outlet per story)

| Label | Meaning |
|--------|--------|
| **Left** | Consistently frames from progressive / labor-aligned editorial stance |
| **Lean left** | Noticeable left-of-center emphasis |
| **Center** | Mixed or institutional “mainstream” balance (still has blind spots) |
| **Lean right** | Noticeable right-of-center emphasis |
| **Right** | Consistently frames from conservative / business-first editorial stance |

**How we use labels**

- Attach **each labeled source** next to its link or in a **Source map** box: outlet name → label → URL.
- When inputs support it, add a **coverage mix** line that reads like data, not argument — e.g. “By outlet count: Left n, Center n, Right n” — **never invent counts**.
- If we disagree with a label for a niche outlet, we say **“label: our desk (provisional)”** and revisit.
- **Wire / agency** copy (e.g. Reuters, AP): treat as **Center** unless a specific piece shows slant; still attribute.

### Ownership & influence (when known)

- Brief **“Who’s behind the coverage”** notes when **verified**: parent company, heavy wire reliance, disclosed donation or ownership patterns. If unknown, **omit** — no filler.

### Blind spots & “what they’re not saying”

- Where review of inputs allows, name **asymmetry** (who’s heavy / who’s quiet) and **gaps** (angles or questions absent from coverage). This is **WDIWF edge** — but **only** when substantiated; do not fabricate blind spots.

### Data > accusation

- Show **how clusters frame** the story (2–3 bullets), **timeline** if useful. Avoid leading with “biased” / “wrong”; let the **map** do the work.

### Center stance, full spectrum

- **Our editorial center** is pro-worker **and** pro-truth: we explain what unions, employers, regulators, and courts are doing without laundering spin.
- For contested topics, we **summarize how left-leaning and right-leaning framings differ**, then **add WDIWF’s own synthesis** — clearly marked as analysis.
- We do not hide disagreement; we **map** it.

### Optional editorial modules (formats vary)

For major items, prefer the full **Signal Check™** structure in [`docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md`](../docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md) §2–§4. Short forms can use:

- **Where the coverage leans** + **what changes by source**  
- **What’s not being said** — substantiated gaps only  
- **Who shapes what you’re reading** — incentive-based, sourced  
- **Signal strength** — **High / Medium / Low** per Signal Check framework §5, aligned with Verified / DEVELOPING below  
- **Decision impact** — *What this should or could change for you this week* (required on flagship items)

## The 4-outlet rule + speed (how to be fast *and* real)

**Default (verified):** A story gets **homepage / main daily lead** treatment only if **at least four reputable, independent outlets or outlets + wire** have published **the same core factual claim** (same event, filing, ruling, survey drop, etc.). Reputable = major national papers, major business/labor desks, established trade press, or AP/Reuters + three others.

**Developing (fast lane):** When the story is breaking and **fewer than four** outlets have it:

- Run it **only** with a visible **DEVELOPING** label.
- State **exactly how many independent sources** we have and **who they are**, with bias labels.
- No implied certainty; no “BREAKING” inflation.
- **Update or elevate** the piece when the fourth qualifying source lands (or when wire confirms and three majors pick up).

**Single-source exclusives:** Rare. Only with **named primary documents** (court PDF, agency docket, verified company statement) and explicit **“Document-backed”** labeling — still not presented as universal truth until corroborated.

**Jackye / WDIWF original reporting:** Clearly tagged **WDIWF Original**; methodology or document basis stated.

## Story elements (every major item)

Signal Check™ maps coverage; **WDIWF adds work stakes and decision impact.** Every major item should **answer** (even if some lines are “not enough data yet — DEVELOPING”):

1. **What happened?** — One factual sentence (then lede expansion).  
2. **Who covered it?** — By **cluster** / family, not only a flat list.  
3. **Facts:** Timestamp, jurisdiction, actor (company, agency, union).  
4. **Source map:** Outlets + bias labels + links (or Primary document link).  
5. **How did framing differ?** — 2–3 bullets (or tight paragraph) left / center / right emphasis.  
6. **What’s missing?** — Blind spots or unanswered Qs **only** if substantiated.  
7. **Spectrum note (if contested):** Same as 5 if not already covered; do not both-sides dignity or rights.  
8. **Who benefits from dominant framing?** — **Jackye’s take** / **Our read** (analysis, signed).  
9. **So what for people at work?** — Decisions, risk, power — the crowded lane **beyond** bias mapping.  
10. **What to watch:** Next date, hearing, earnings call, negotiation milestone.  
11. **Signal strength:** **High / Medium / Low** (see [`docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md`](../docs/SIGNAL_CHECK_FRAMEWORK.md) §5) — consistent with Verified vs DEVELOPING.  
12. **Decision impact:** *What this should or could change for you this week* — one concrete, actionable line (Signal Check™ §2).

---

## Corrections

- Fix fast, show the fix, note **what changed** at the bottom of the piece. No stealth edits on material facts.

---

## Competitive moat

**Speed + source literacy + Jackye’s lens** — not hot takes without receipts. The reader leaves knowing **what happened**, **how coverage clustered and differed**, **what was absent from the conversation**, **who’s behind the bylines when it matters**, **what this means for people at work**, and **what to do this week** (**Signal Check™** **decision impact**) — without confusion about fact vs. framing vs. analysis.
