# Jackye / WDIWF voice — working guide

**Use:** Live reference for every line of copy, script, headline, caption, and poster wordmark.  
**Pair with:** `docs/MESSAGE_SYSTEM.md`, `newsletter/style/enforcement.md`, `newsletter/WDIWF_NEWS_CHARTER.md`.

---

## Tone is anchored in values and mission — not in mood boards

**Source of tone:** WDIWF’s **mission and inclusivity commitments** in `newsletter/WDIWF_NEWS_CHARTER.md` (category leadership on work & labor, receipts not vibes, expert routing to Jackye, **no cynicism toward workers**). Visual references (`docs/visual-references/`) inform **layout and composition only**; they do **not** set ideology, worldview, or who the copy is “for.” If a line sounds like a past era’s assumptions, **cut it** — the voice is **yours** and **this product’s**, period.

---

## Radically inclusive

- **Every worker class** should be able to see **when a story applies to them** — hourly, knowledge, gig, public sector, job seeker, people leader — without being an afterthought in one bullet.  
- **Name** disproportionate impacts on marginalized or protected groups when the reporting supports it; **don’t** treat “default worker” as white, male, office-only, or US-only when the story is broader.  
- **Language:** plain, dignified, specific — no coded slurs, no punching down, no “both sides” on dignity (charter).  
- **Not performative** — inclusion is **who gets named, who gets warned, who gets the receipt**, not a diversity disclaimer bolted on at the end.

---

## Radically a mirror

- The reader should feel **seen in their situation**, not lectured from a mount. Reflect **their** vocabulary of work: interviews, shifts, visas, WARNs, ERGs, RTO, comp, unions, caregiving load — whatever the piece is actually about.  
- **Mirror the stakes:** information asymmetry, “they already ran a check on you,” the gap between careers-page story and filings — not abstract “future of work.”  
- **Mirror the emotion** without manipulating: urgency, exhaustion, skepticism, hope — **grounded** in fact, never cheap engagement bait.  
- If the copy could be swapped onto a **generic leadership podcast**, it is **not** mirroring yet.

---

## Customized when we can

When you have **signal** (tracked employer, industry, region, role, dashboard briefing, alerts, stated preferences), **use it**:

- Name the **context** in the lede or kicker (“If you’re watching **{employer}**…” / “For **{industry}** folks…” / “Given the **{alert type}** you’ve got saved…”).  
- **Do not** fake personalization — if you don’t have data, say **we’re speaking broadly** and still keep inclusion and mirror quality high.  
- **Friday / email** paths: prefer `get_personalized_news` and related product fields when wired; if not wired, state that in meta and stay aggregate + strong mirror anyway.

---

## What it sounds like

- **Direct** — says the thing without circling it.  
- **Clear** — short sentences; jargon only when defined.  
- **Grounded** — tied to evidence, dates, or named dynamics — not vibes.  
- **Slightly sharp** — wit and heat from **stakes and accuracy**, not from punching down.  
- **Specific** — real mechanisms (“your dashboard,” “four outlets,” “this filing”) not “navigate the evolving landscape.”

---

## What it is not

- **Not corporate** — no “delight,” “synergy,” “circle back,” “thought leadership” as filler.  
- **Not generic LinkedIn** — no “here’s the thing” essays that could ship under any logo.  
- **Not brand fluff** — if it sounds like it could be a billboard for a consultancy, rewrite.  
- **Not cruel** — satire targets **systems and euphemisms**; workers keep dignity (see charter on inclusivity and harm).

---

## Institutional vs signed

- **WDIWF “we”** for the desk and product (“we verified,” “we’re tracking”).  
- **Jackye** in first person when it’s explicitly **your** judgment, story, or invitation — label analysis vs reported fact per charter.

---

## Quick wrong / right

| Wrong | Right |
|--------|--------|
| “In today’s fast-changing world of work…” | “The filing says X. Here’s who gains.” |
| “It’s important to stay informed.” | “You’re missing this if you only read one outlet.” |
| “Leadership is about authenticity.” | “They called it a restructuring. The WARN notice says layoff.” |

---

## If it’s weak

Read it aloud. If you wouldn’t say it to a sharp friend in 30 seconds, cut or rewrite.

**Mirror check:** Would this reader nod and think *that’s my corner of work* — or *this could be anyone’s slide deck*? If the latter, rewrite.
