## 🔵 LinkedIn

Before your first interview, a company has already run a background check, verified your employment history, reviewed your credit, sourced your references, and cross-referenced your digital footprint.

You've Googled them and checked their Glassdoor rating.

That is not a fair fight.

This is the information asymmetry I think about every single day. And it's exactly why I'm building WDIWF.

Career decisions are some of the biggest financial and personal decisions you'll ever make. We're talking about where you spend a third of your life, who shapes your growth, what kind of culture you absorb. And most people are making those decisions with *significantly* less information than they'd use to book a hotel.

That's not a personal failing. That's a structural gap. The intelligence exists — it's just been hoarded on one side of the table.

What I'm building with Who Do I Work For is the layer that puts real intelligence in your hands before you decide. Not anonymous reviews. Not PR-managed "employer branding." Actual signals — culture patterns, leadership consistency, team data, the real story behind the job post.

The companies that are genuinely great? They'll welcome the light.

The ones that won't? That tells you everything you need to know.

Your next move doesn't have to be a leap of faith. It can be a strategy. And that shift — from hoping to *knowing* — is what I'm here to make possible for every person who's ever taken a job and thought, "wait, this is not what they sold me."

You deserved better intel. It's coming. 💫
