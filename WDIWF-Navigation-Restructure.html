Why These Names Work

"Receipts" is clever and on-brand for your voice, but it only makes sense if you already know what the product does. Same problem with "My Intel" and "Signals." New visitors don't have context for metaphors — they need outcomes.

Companies (not "Career Map")

Users come to search a company. Call it what it is. "Career Map" sounds like a self-help exercise, not an employer intelligence dossier.

Compare (not "Compare Companies")

Shorter. Everyone knows what compare means. The context is already established by the product.

Offers (not "Offer Analysis")

Action-oriented. "I have an offer — now what?" This is the decision moment your whole product builds toward.

Watchlist (not "My Intel")

"My Intel" sounds like a spy movie. "Watchlist" is instantly understood — it's your tracked companies and alerts.

The Viral Loop

Your viral moment isn't "career map." It's when someone shares a company score. Build the product around these shareable moments:

  • "I ran my offer through WDIWF and found 6 red flags before I signed."
  • "This company scored 4.2 out of 10. Here's why."
  • "Check the employer dossier before you quit your job for them."
  • "I compared both companies side by side — one was a 3.1, the other an 8.4."
  • "My job offer looked great until I checked the leadership churn rate."

Homepage headline recommendation: "See what the offer hides." Then subhead: "Check pay risk, leadership churn, hiring patterns, and employee signals before you accept." CTA: "Analyze a Company."

What to Do Next

Step 1: Simplify the Nav

Collapse 4 navigation systems (TopBar, AppSidebar, MarketingNav, ContextSidebar) into ONE. Five tabs. Done.

Step 2: Merge the Offer Pages

You have 7 separate offer-related pages (offer-check, offer-review, offer-review-direct, offer-clarity, strategic-offer-review, negotiation-simulator, decision-engine). Make it ONE flow.

Step 3: Unify Company Pages

Company Profile + Dossier + Receipts + Intelligence Report + Board Intelligence + One-Pager = ONE Company Report with tabs.

Step 4: Hide, Don't Delete

Keep the routes alive (don't break links) but remove them from navigation. Redirect old URLs to their new homes.
