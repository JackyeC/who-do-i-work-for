My honest take: "Who Do I Work For" is already a strong brand name. The problem was never the name — it's the navigation and page structure underneath it. WDIWF is provocative, memorable, and tells the story. You don't need to rename the whole product. You need to clean up what's inside it.

The Viral Loop (Both Sources)

Both Perplexity and I identified the same thing: the viral moment isn't a page name. It's when someone shares a company score or offer red flag.

  • "I ran my offer through WDIWF and found 6 red flags before I signed."
  • "This company scored 4.2 out of 10. Here's why."
  • "Check the employer dossier before you quit your job for them."
  • "I compared both companies side by side — one was a 3.1, the other an 8.4."
  • "My job offer looked great until I checked the leadership churn rate."

Build for sharing, not browsing. Every Company Report should have a one-click share card with the clarity score, risk level, and one-line verdict. That card is your marketing engine — not a blog, not a job board, not an HR Tech Intel page.

Execution Steps

Step 1: One Nav System

Kill AppSidebar, ContextSidebar, and MarketingNav. Keep one TopBar with 5 tabs + More + Account. This is a code change in 4 files.

Step 2: Merge Company Pages

Company Profile + Dossier + Receipts + Intelligence + Board Intel + One-Pager = ONE Company Report with tabs. Currently 6+ routes → 1 route with internal tabs.

Step 3: Merge Offer Pages

7 separate offer pages → 1 guided flow. Enter details → Risk review → Negotiation flags → Decision. One route, step-by-step.

Step 4: Remove from Nav, Keep Routes

The 40+ pages being cut stay alive as URLs (don't break links). Remove them from all navigation. Add redirects to new homes.

Step 5: Homepage Rewrite

Company search as the hero. One headline. One CTA. One action. Kill the feature tour — just let people search.

Step 6: Share Cards

Every Company Report generates a shareable card (image + score + verdict). This is your viral engine. Build it into the report page.
