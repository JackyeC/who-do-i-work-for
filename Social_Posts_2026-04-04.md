## 💼 LinkedIn

Here's a hot take: companies know more about you before your first interview than you'll know about them after six months on the job.

They have your LinkedIn profile, your social media history, background checks, reference networks, Glassdoor scrapes, salary benchmarks, and internal talent intelligence tools — all before you've even shaken a hand.

And you? You have a careers page that hasn't been updated since 2022 and a Glassdoor rating that five disgruntled employees tanked two years ago.

This isn't a complaint. It's a design problem.

The workforce was built on information asymmetry. Companies win when you don't know what you don't know — what the real culture is, what your colleagues actually earn, what the turnover looks like six months after your onboarding glow wears off.

That asymmetry is ending.

Not because companies suddenly got generous with data. But because we're building the tools to close the gap. Career intelligence, not job boards. Receipts, not reviews. Data that helps you walk in knowing what you're actually walking into.

Your next move shouldn't feel like a prayer. It should feel like power.

That's what Who Do I Work For is building — and we're just getting started.
