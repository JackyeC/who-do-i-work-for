The Formula

📊

Bloomberg

Lives in the layout

Dark background. Amber accent. Monospace data. Tight grids. Information density that feels authoritative, not cluttered. The structure says: this is serious intelligence, not a blog.

💛

Oprah

Lives in the words

Warm serif headlines. "I see you" energy. Every data point connects back to *your* career, *your* decision, *your* power. The copy says: you deserve to know this.

🔥

RHOBH

Lives in the content

Receipts. Drama. The evidence trail. Red flags that make you gasp. The content says: we have the receipts, and we're not afraid to show them.

Color System

Bloomberg's signature is amber-on-black — not hot pink. The current WDIWF pink (#ff4d6a) reads "consumer social app." The shift to amber immediately adds 10 years of credibility and signals financial intelligence.

Where Each Influence Lives

Bloomberg

Structure & Data

Dark background (#0C0C0C). Amber accent (#F5A623). Monospace for all data (JetBrains Mono). Tight data grids. Scrolling ticker. Source citations on everything. Information density. No whitespace waste.

Components: Ticker bar, data grid cells, sidebar metrics, source citations, score displays, risk badges, tab navigation.

Oprah

Voice & Warmth

Serif headlines (Playfair Display). Warm amber glow, not cold blue. Every verdict speaks to YOU. "You're not joining a steady system." "You deserve to know this." The data has a soul.

Components: Verdict callouts, company report headlines, homepage headline, onboarding copy, CTA language, share card verdicts.

RHOBH

Drama & Evidence

Red-flagged receipt cards with left border accents. "Evidence" not "data." EEOC filings, WARN notices, leadership exits — presented like investigation results. The drama is in the truth.

Components: Receipt/evidence cards, red flag alerts, "Recent Evidence" sections, whistleblower-style source formatting, risk scoring language.

What Changes from Today

CURRENT

Hot pink accent color

Reads consumer/social. Undercuts the intelligence positioning.

NEW

Bloomberg amber

Reads financial intelligence. Still warm (not cold corporate blue). Instant credibility upgrade.

CURRENT

Sans-serif everything

Generic SaaS feel. No editorial warmth. Could be any dashboard.

NEW

Serif headlines + mono data + sans body

Serif adds editorial gravitas (Oprah/NYT). Mono adds data authority (Bloomberg). Sans handles the UI.

CURRENT

Data presented flat

Numbers without context or hierarchy. All data looks the same.

NEW

Data in Bloomberg-style grid cells

Each metric in its own cell with label, value, and directional change. Instantly scannable.

CURRENT

Evidence mixed into page content

EEOC filings, WARN notices, and leadership changes are buried in long pages.

NEW

Evidence as "receipt cards"

Each piece of evidence is a card with a red/amber/blue left border, source citation, and filing date. RHOBH receipts energy — organized like a case file.

CURRENT

Big score, no verdict

4.2/10 is visible but there's no human-readable interpretation.

NEW

Score + warm, direct verdict

"High opportunity, high volatility. You're not joining a steady system." The number gets a voice. That's Oprah.
